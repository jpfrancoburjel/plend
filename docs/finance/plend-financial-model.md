# Plend Financial Model

## Purpose
Estimate whether Plend can work economically in Gavà Mar and Castelldefels as a small premium local service.

This is a first operating model, not fake certainty.

---

# 1. Core business assumptions

## Offer structure
Plend will sell:
- decoration-only packages
- soft play / game station rental packages
- bundled full-service birthday packages

## Operational assumptions
Each event may include:
- prep and loading
- travel to site
- setup
- on-site supervision
- teardown
- return transport
- cleaning / reset / storage

That means each booking is a **mini operation**, not a passive rental.

---

# 2. Startup investment model

## Lean launch investment
### Inventory and decor assets
- soft play modules and toddler game units: **€3,500**
- ball pit / mats / small play accessories: **€1,200**
- reusable decor inventory for 2 to 3 themes: **€2,000**
- signage, branding, personalization basics: **€500**
- cleaning, bins, covers, maintenance items: **€500**

### Logistics and setup
- vehicle adaptation / transport setup: **€1,000**
- storage setup or initial storage cost: **€1,200**
- legal / insurance / admin basics: **€800**

### Commercial setup
- branding + logo + visual system: **€500**
- website / landing page / photography / social content: **€1,500**
- launch marketing budget: **€800**

## Lean startup total
- **€13,500**

## Tighter startup version
If you keep fewer themes and simpler decor:
- **€8,000 to €10,500**

## More premium launch version
If you want stronger design and more inventory early:
- **€15,000 to €22,000**

## Recommendation
Start in the **€9k to €13.5k** band.
That is enough to look serious without overcommitting capital.

# 3. Pricing assumptions

## Price bands
### Decoration only
- average ticket assumption: **€250**

### Soft play / game station only
- average ticket assumption: **€350**

### Full package
- average ticket assumption: **€650**

### Premium full package
- average ticket assumption: **€950**

## Mix assumption for a realistic early business
For a small launch, the average blended ticket can be modeled at:
- **€550** base case

That assumes most bookings are full-service or partial bundles, not cheap decor-only jobs.

# 5. Monthly fixed costs

## Fixed monthly structure
- storage: **€250**
- insurance/admin/software/phone: **€120**
- ongoing marketing: **€250**
- vehicle baseline/fuel overhead not attached to one event: **€180**
- miscellaneous reserve: **€100**

## Monthly fixed cost estimate
- **€900/month**

Could be lower if storage is already available.
Could be higher if transport or paid marketing is heavier.

---

# 6. Break-even analysis

## Break-even on monthly fixed costs only
If average contribution per booking is about **€300**, then:
- €900 fixed costs / €300 contribution = **3 bookings/month** to cover monthly fixed costs

## Break-even on startup recovery
If startup investment is **€10,000** and average contribution is **€300**, then:
- €10,000 / €300 = about **34 bookings** to recover startup capital

If the startup is **€13,500**, then:
- €13,500 / €300 = about **45 bookings**

## Practical read
That means the business starts feeling real when it can maintain:
- at least **4 to 6 bookings/month**
- with average ticket and contribution holding up

# 7. Monthly sales scenarios

## Conservative
- 2 events/month
- average ticket: **€400**
- revenue: **€800**
- contribution estimate: **€400 to €500**
- result after fixed costs: likely **negative**

## Base case
- 5 events/month
- average ticket: **€550**
- revenue: **€2,750**
- contribution estimate: **~€1,500**
- after fixed costs: **~€600** before taxes and founder salary

## Good traction case
- 8 events/month
- average ticket: **€650**
- revenue: **€5,200**
- contribution estimate: **~€2,880**
- after fixed costs: **~€1,980** before taxes and founder salary

## Strong local premium case
- 10 events/month
- average ticket: **€750**
- revenue: **€7,500**
- contribution estimate: **~€4,000+**
- after fixed costs: potentially healthy for a small operator

# 8. Annual view

## First-year realistic path
Assume:
- first 3 months are slow ramp
- then 3 to 5 average bookings/month
- stronger spring/summer weekends

### Realistic first-year revenue band
- **€20,000 to €40,000**

### Better first-year band if positioning lands
- **€40,000 to €60,000**

That stronger band needs:
- polished branding
- reliable word of mouth
- good photo/video content
- repeatable ops

---

# 9. What breaks the model

## 1. Underpricing
If the blended average ticket falls below about **€400**, the business gets ugly fast.

## 2. Too much labor per event
If setup/teardown is chaotic, your margin gets eaten by time.

## 3. Too many low-value custom requests
Custom work can feel premium but actually be margin poison.

## 4. Weak booking density
A few random events per month is not enough.
You need enough local density to make transport/setup worthwhile.

## 5. Damage / cleaning overhead
Anything for small kids gets messy. If this is underestimated, your real margin drops.

---

# 10. Financial recommendation

## Green-light condition
Proceed if you believe you can achieve within a few months:
- **4 to 6 bookings/month**
- average ticket around **€500 to €650**
- clean operations and reusable inventory

## Yellow-light condition
Proceed carefully if:
- you expect only 2 to 3 bookings/month for too long
- average ticket may stay under €400

## Best move
Start small but premium:
- 2 to 3 launch themes
- 1 to 2 game station setups
- aggressive focus on average order value
- no cheap custom chaos

That is the only sane path.
